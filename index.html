Creating your own Young Adult baby

We all have a main character in our story so we should make sure we use them to the best of our abilities. The main character is the most important element of the story. We have to find balance with them; we can't spoil or abuse them. For example, in summer camp Eugene provides his feeling on his situation he is in. Its how the audience enters the world. We see through our protagonist's eyes and sometimes even become them. As writers, we have to make sure that we bring this character to life for the sake of our reader’s enjoyment, and sometimes ours.

Build A YA World

Another important story point is how your characters live in their worlds. The place where we all live and see every single day. If you are confused about your world then your audience will be just as confused if not more. Think of your favorite stories, movies, comics, etc where do they all take place at? Can you picture living in that world? Setting brings the story to life so as writers we have to make this one of our top priorities.

Building your monstrosity of a short story

This is where the madness begins. It’s a short story. A short story. You don’t have to make this story an epic novel just enough words to tell a story. Some people prefer short stories mostly due to their attention span. Having a story that is straight to the point offers satisfaction for the readers. Keep in mind your word count. While many stories do not follow this rule, it is essential that the story has no more than 8,000 words. As far young adult stories go your monstrosity should be wrapped up nicely into this short story.
